## 3. Main theorem

### Theorem 565.1 — echosahedral source-selector theorem

Let `(E_r)` be a cofinal carrier lineage satisfying E1–E5. Then, at every stage `r`:

#### (a) Canonical twelve-unit split

The coefficient module has the canonical decomposition

\[
V_r=\bigoplus_{p\in P_r}\mathbb R e_{r,p}.
\]

On the total-charge fiber `Q_r`, `H_r` has the unique minimizer

\[
q_p=1\quad\text{for every }p,
\]

with

\[
\min H_r=12,
\qquad
\min_{q\ne\mathbf1}H_r=14.
\]

Hence the exact unit-splitting gap is

\[
\delta_{\mathrm{unit}}=2.
\]

#### (b) Canonical inverse pairing

For every `p`, there is exactly one port at distance three. Define

\[
\iota_r(p)=\text{the unique }q\text{ with }d_r(p,q)=3.
\]

Then `iota_r` is a unique fixed-point-free involution and gives six unordered axes

\[
A_r=P_r/\langle\iota_r\rangle,
\qquad |A_r|=6.
\]

Every incidence automorphism commutes with `iota_r`.

#### (c) Source-derived `A5`

The full incidence automorphism group has order 120. Coherent face orientation defines a surjective character

\[
\varepsilon_r:\operatorname{Aut}(P_r,E_r,F_r)\to\{\pm1\}.
\]

Its positive kernel has order 60. It contains exactly five Klein-four subgroups. Conjugation on those five subgroups is faithful and its image is all 60 even permutations of five objects. Therefore

\[
\operatorname{Aut}^+(P_r,E_r,F_r^+)\cong A_5.
\]

The antipode is central, reverses orientation, and the full group is

\[
\operatorname{Aut}(P_r,E_r,F_r)
 \cong A_5\times C_2.
\]

The `A5` action is faithful and transitive on the twelve ports and faithful and transitive on the six axes. An axis stabilizer has order ten.

#### (d) Canonical rank-three frame

Let `d_r` be graph distance and set `t=1/sqrt(5)`. Define the exact `12 x 12` matrix

\[
G_{pq}=\begin{cases}
1,&p=q,\\
 t,&d_r(p,q)=1,\\
-t,&d_r(p,q)=2,\\
-1,&d_r(p,q)=3.
\end{cases}
\]

Then

\[
G^T=G,
\qquad G^2=4G,
\qquad \operatorname{tr}G=12.
\]

Consequently `G` is positive semidefinite of rank three and is the Gram matrix of twelve unit vectors spanning `R^3`. The realization is unique up to `O(3)`. The oriented face class reduces the equivalence to `SO(3)`.

For every incidence automorphism `g`,

\[
P_g^TGP_g=G.
\]

The induced determinant on the three-dimensional Gram image equals the combinatorial orientation character. Thus the positive subgroup acts through `SO(3)` and gives port-frame equivariance.

Moreover,

\[
v_{\iota(p)}=-v_p,
\qquad
(v_p\cdot v_q)^2=\frac15
\]

for distinct axes. The six axes are therefore the unique real equiangular tight frame `ETF(3,6)`, equivalently the six axes of a regular icosahedron, and the twelve signed vectors form its vertex orbit.

#### (e) Refinement and relabelling naturality

For every declared lineage map `c_sr`,

\[
c_{sr}(\mathbb R e_{r,p})=\mathbb R e_{s,c_{sr}(p)},
\]

\[
c_{sr}\circ\iota_r=\iota_s\circ c_{sr},
\]

and

\[
P_{c_{sr}}^TG_sP_{c_{sr}}=G_r.
\]

The maps lie in the positive `A5` action and satisfy the tower cocycle. Therefore the unit lines, inverse pairs, axes, group action, and port frame descend to one cofinal refinement class.

If all source fields are relabelled by an arbitrary permutation `sigma`, then every derived object is conjugated by `sigma`; the canonical oriented-incidence hash, group invariants, frame class, and refinement theorem remain unchanged.

## 4. Proof

### Lemma 565.2 — primitive atoms give the only source-visible line split

Because the `e_p` are the minimal nonzero central idempotents of `Z_r^port`, every algebra automorphism permutes them. Therefore

\[
V_r=\bigoplus_p\mathbb R e_p
\]

is intrinsic to the quotient-visible algebra. Any purported source-natural one-dimensional decomposition must be permuted by every port-algebra isomorphism; after identifying each line with its central support, it is the same unordered family.

This is the datum that Euler incidence alone lacked. The conclusion is not that every abstract 12-dimensional vector space has a preferred basis; it is that the simulator’s twelve primitive port events supply one.

### Lemma 565.3 — exact unit floor

For `q in Q_r`, write `x_p=q_p-1`. Since `sum q_p=12`,

\[
\sum_px_p=0.
\]

Then

\[
H(q)=\sum_p(1+x_p)^2
=12+2\sum_px_p+\sum_px_p^2
=12+\sum_px_p^2.
\]

Thus `H(q)>=12`, with equality only when all `x_p=0`. If `x` is a nonzero integral zero-sum vector, it has at least one positive and one negative entry. Hence

\[
\sum_px_p^2\ge1^2+(-1)^2=2.
\]

The bound is attained by moving one unit from one port to another, so the next floor is exactly 14.

### Lemma 565.4 — antipode

The exact all-pairs breadth-first search gives, from every port, one vertex at distance zero, five at distance one, five at distance two, and one at distance three. Hence `iota` is defined everywhere and has no fixed point.

Distance symmetry gives `d(p,iota(p))=3`. The unique distance-three point from `iota(p)` is therefore `p`, so `iota^2=id`. If `g` preserves incidence, it preserves graph distance; uniqueness yields

\[
g\iota(p)=\iota g(p).
\]

No second incidence-natural inverse pairing exists.

### Lemma 565.5 — finite group identification

The verifier enumerates every bijection preserving the exact distance matrix. There are 120. Every such bijection preserves the unoriented face complex. Comparing a face with the declared coherent orientation gives a homomorphism to `{+1,-1}`, with 60 elements of each sign.

For the positive subgroup, exact conjugacy enumeration gives class/order data

\[
1^1,\qquad 2^{15},\qquad 3^{20},\qquad 5^{24}.
\]

The verifier then avoids any classification-table assumption. It constructs every commuting triple of involutions and finds exactly five subgroups isomorphic to `C2 x C2`. Conjugation permutes these five subgroups. The kernel is trivial, all images are even, and there are 60 distinct images. Since the alternating group on five letters has exactly 60 elements, the image is precisely `A5`.

The distance-three involution is itself an automorphism. It is central and lies in the negative coset. Multiplication by it maps the positive subgroup bijectively to the negative coset, proving the direct-product statement.

### Lemma 565.6 — exact Gram projector

The distance relations partition ordered port pairs, so `G` is determined by incidence alone. Direct exact multiplication in `Q(sqrt(5))` gives

\[
G^2=4G.
\]

Because `G` is real symmetric, each eigenvalue satisfies `lambda^2=4lambda`, hence is 0 or 4. Its trace is 12, so 4 has multiplicity three and 0 multiplicity nine. Thus `G` is positive semidefinite, rank three, and has a real factorization

\[
G=V^TV,
\qquad V\in\mathbb R^{3\times12}.
\]

The diagonal entries give unit columns. Rank three means the columns span. Two spanning factorizations of the same Gram matrix differ by one orthogonal transformation.

If `d(p,q)=3`, then two unit vectors have inner product `-1`, so they are negatives. For ports on different antipodal axes the inner products are `+/-1/sqrt(5)`, giving squared line inner product `1/5`.

Every distance isometry satisfies `P_g^T G P_g=G`; hence it induces an orthogonal map on the Gram image. To certify handedness without choosing coordinates, set `E=G/4`. For the induced three-dimensional action `R_g`,

\[
\operatorname{tr}(R_g^k)=\operatorname{tr}(P_g^kE)
=\frac14\operatorname{tr}(P_g^kG),
\qquad k=1,2,3.
\]

Newton’s identity gives

\[
\det R_g=
\frac{t_1^3-3t_1t_2+2t_3}{6}.
\]

The exact receipt obtains `+1` on all 60 positively oriented automorphisms and `-1` on all 60 negatively oriented automorphisms. Thus the combinatorial and spatial orientation characters agree.

### Lemma 565.7 — refinement descent

A lineage map preserves primitive atoms, incidence, and oriented faces. It therefore preserves graph distance and the formula defining `G`. By Lemma 565.4 it commutes with `iota`, and by Lemma 565.6 it intertwines the frame. Since it preserves face orientation, it lies in the positive subgroup. The declared cocycle makes these stagewise intertwiners a coherent cofinal object.

### Completion of Theorem 565.1

Parts (a)–(e) are Lemmas 565.2–565.7. Every construction is a deterministic function of E1–E4, and E5 rejects downstream target data. This proves the source-only and uniqueness claims.

## 6. Hypothesis-removal classification and countermodels

The theorem does not hide necessity claims. Each load-bearing field has a finite countermodel or a classified larger family.

### N1. Remove primitive port atoms

Keep only an unmarked real 12-space. One-dimensional decompositions are no longer canonical; their presentations form the continuum

\[
GL(12,\mathbb R)\big/\big((\mathbb R^*)^{12}\rtimes S_{12}\big).
\]

The finite mutant with only six declared atoms is rejected as `PORT_ATOMS_COUNT`.

### N2. Remove equal trace weights

Let normalized weights be

\[
w_0=\frac4{15},\qquad w_i=\frac1{15}\ (i>0),
\]

and use `H_w(q)=sum w_i q_i^2`. The all-one vector has cost 1, while

\[
q=(0,2,1,\ldots,1)
\]

has total 12 and cost `14/15`. The equal unit split is no longer selected. The finite schema mutant is rejected as `PORT_TRACE_NOT_UNIFORM`.

### N3. Remove the total-twelve condition

At total 13, the quadratic minimizers are the twelve vectors with one coordinate 2 and the other eleven coordinates 1. At total 11, the twelve minimizers have one coordinate 0. Thus the singleton unit allocation is specific to total 12. The finite mutant is rejected as `TOTAL_CHARGE_NOT_12`.

### N4. Remove strict quadratic convexity

For nonnegative integer `q` with total 12 and linear cost `sum q_i`, every weak composition minimizes. Their number is

\[
\binom{23}{11}=1,352,078.
\]

The finite mutant is rejected as `COST_NOT_STRICT_QUADRATIC`.

### N5. Remove incidence

Twelve labelled units alone admit

\[
\frac{12!}{2^6 6!}=10,395
\]

fixed-point-free pairings, with no canonical choice. They also admit the full `S12` permutation group rather than a selected icosahedral action. Deleting one source edge is rejected as `EDGE_COUNT`.

### N6. Keep the graph but remove coherent face orientation

The graph still determines antipodes and the rank-three unoriented frame, but the surviving symmetry is the full order-120 group

\[
A_5\times C_2.
\]

There is no source distinction between the two handed frame classes. An orientation-reversing lineage is rejected as `REFINEMENT_INCIDENCE`.

### N7. Remove incidence-natural refinement

A transposition of two port labels can be a bijection but does not preserve the carrier incidence, antipode, or frame. The finite mutant is rejected as `REFINEMENT_INCIDENCE`.

### N8. Remove the refinement cocycle

On three stages `r0 < r1 < r2`, choose valid positive maps on `r0->r1` and `r1->r2`, but set the direct `r0->r2` map to a different positive automorphism. Every pairwise map is locally admissible, yet no refinement descent exists. The finite mutant is rejected as `REFINEMENT_COCYCLE`.

### N9. Remove the source firewall

A selector can be forced to return any desired downstream representation by placing that target in the manifest. Such a family has no source-only evidential content. The finite target-injection mutant is rejected as `FORBIDDEN_DEPENDENCY`.

The generated JSON bundle records all nine finite controls and the four classified larger families.
